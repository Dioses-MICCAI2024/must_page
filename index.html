MuST: Multi-Scale Transformers for Surgical Phase Recognition

Alejandra Pérez1, Santiago Rodríguez1, Nicolás Ayobi2, Nicolás Aparicio2, Eugénie Dessevres1, Pablo Arbeláez1
1Universidad de los Andes, 2BCV-Uniandes
Paper arXiv

Multi-Scale Transformers for Surgical Phase Recognition in videos using short-, mid-, and long-term context.

Abstract

Phase recognition in surgical videos is crucial for enhancing computer-aided surgical systems. Existing methods often rely on fixed temporal windows, struggling to capture short-, mid-, and long-term information. We propose Multi-Scale Transformers for Surgical Phase Recognition (MuST), a novel approach that captures multi-scale temporal context using a Transformer-based framework.

Our method introduces a Multi-Term Frame Encoder and a Temporal Consistency Module to model short-, mid-, and long-term temporal dependencies, enabling robust surgical phase recognition across variable phase durations. We demonstrate the superiority of MuST on multiple surgical benchmarks.
